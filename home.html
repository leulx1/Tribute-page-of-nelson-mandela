Nelson Mandela

The symbol of hope and justice

The Life Time of Nelson Mandela

  • 1918: Born Rolihlahla Mandela in Mvezo, South Africa, into the Thembu royal family.
  • 1940s: Studies law at the University of Fort Hare and Witwatersrand, becoming politicized by the injustices of apartheid. Joins the African National Congress (ANC) in 1944 and co-founds its militant youth league, campaigning for racial equality.
  • 1950s: Leads non-violent protests and civil disobedience against apartheid laws. The ANC is outlawed in 1961, and Mandela goes underground.
  • 1962:Arrested for sabotage and conspiracy to overthrow the government. Sentenced to life imprisonment on Robben Island, enduring harsh conditions and solitary confinement
  • 1980s: Becomes an international symbol of the anti-apartheid movement. Despite years in prison, his message of peace and reconciliation resonates worldwide.
  • 1990: Released from prison after 27 years due to mounting international pressure. Holds historic talks with then-South African President F.W. de Klerk, advocating for a peaceful transition to democracy.
  • 1993: Awarded the Nobel Peace Prize alongside de Klerk for their efforts to dismantle apartheid.
  • 1994: Leads the ANC to victory in South Africa's first free and fair elections, becoming the country's first black president. Works to dismantle apartheid's legacy and promote racial reconciliation.
  • 1999: Steps down as president after one term, focusing on promoting peace and social justice globally.
  • 2013: Passes away at the age of 95, leaving behind a legacy of courage, forgiveness, and unwavering dedication to freedom.
"Nelson Mandela was a tower of strength for justice and human dignity. His life's work is a testament to the power of courage, patience, and reconciliation."

--- Barack Obama, Former US President
